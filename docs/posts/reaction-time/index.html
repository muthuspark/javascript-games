FAQ

The average human reaction time is around 250-300 milliseconds. Anything under 200ms is considered excellent. Professional gamers and athletes often have reaction times between 150-200ms.

Regular practice helps improve reaction time. Getting enough sleep, staying hydrated, and reducing distractions can also help. Some studies suggest that playing action video games may improve reaction times.

Many factors affect reaction time including age, fatigue, caffeine intake, and even the time of day. Visual reaction times are typically faster than auditory ones. Your reaction time may also improve with practice on this specific test.
